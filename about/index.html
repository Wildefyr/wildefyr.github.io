Other than my main machine, I'm currently running this server via DigitalOcean as the github student developer pack was kind enough to give a whopping $100 credit to any prospective student who has a University email. I also have a Acer C720 Chromebook for portable development which again runs Arch Linux. It's a fantastic little piece of hardware for the price and I would whole heartedly recommend it.

I currently have two mechanical keyboards, the first one being a CoolerMaster QuickFire TK with Cherry MX blues, used primarily for playing RTS and MOBA games. I don't like the feel of the blues for typing for long periods of time as I find the keys slightly too heavy for my liking. Enter the brand new Poker 3, overall a much smaller keyboard as it's a 60%. However, it's got Cherry MX reds, which are slightly lighter than blues, at 45G to the blues 55G. It's a different switch type overall, as the pressure while pressing the key remains equal all the way to the bottom. The Poker 3 also has the capability to record key presses and replay them back, much like Vim's recording feature. However, it's implemented on the keyboard itself and thus can be used across machines. Pretty handy. I primarily use it with the chromebook when I am out and about!
